Causes Of Climate Change

1. Burning Fossil Fuels

The largest contributor to climate change is the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas for energy. These activities release massive amounts of carbon dioxide (CO₂) and other greenhouse gases, trapping heat in the atmosphere and raising global temperatures.

2. Deforestation And Land Use Change

Forests absorb CO₂, playing a crucial role in balancing the Earth’s climate. However, large-scale deforestation for agriculture, logging, and urban development reduces this capacity, increasing greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

3. Industrial Activities

Manufacturing processes release greenhouse gases such as methane, nitrous oxide, and CO₂. Industries like cement, steel, and chemical production also consume large amounts of energy, contributing to the crisis.

4. Agriculture and Livestock

Agricultural activities, particularly the raising of livestock, emit high levels of methane (a potent greenhouse gas). Fertilizers also release nitrous oxide, which significantly contributes to warming.

5. Waste and Pollution

Poor waste management leads to methane emissions from landfills, and the production and disposal of single-use plastics pollute both land and oceans, disrupting ecosystems and amplifying climate risks.

6. Transportation

Vehicles powered by fossil fuels emit significant amounts of CO₂. With more cars, planes, and ships in use worldwide, transportation remains a major source of global emissions.
